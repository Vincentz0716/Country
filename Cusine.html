Cusine

Brunost, or brown cheese, is a traditional Norwegian cheese made from caramelized whey, typically from goat's milk. It has a sweet, slightly tangy flavor and a smooth, brown appearance. Often served in thin slices on bread or crackers, it’s a beloved part of Norwegian cuisine.

Rokt Las, a gem from Norway, serves dishes blending local flavors and modern flair. Enjoy smoked salmon, reindeer stew, and cloudberry desserts, celebrating Norway's bounty!

Kjøttboller are traditional Norwegian meatballs, seasoned with spices like nutmeg and allspice for a warm, savory flavor. Typically made with a mix of beef and pork, they’re served with creamy brown gravy, boiled potatoes, lingonberry jam, and vegetables. This hearty dish is a staple of Norwegian cuisine, offering comfort and tradition in every bite.

Lefse is a traditional Norwegian flatbread made from potatoes, flour, butter, and cream. Soft and slightly sweet, it’s typically rolled thin and cooked on a griddle. Often enjoyed with butter and sugar, it can also be paired with savory fillings like smoked salmon or cheese. Lefse is a beloved staple of Norwegian cuisine, especially during holidays, bringing a comforting taste of tradition to any meal.

Pinnekjøtt is a classic Norwegian dish made of salted and dried lamb ribs, traditionally steamed over birch sticks, which give it a distinctive smoky aroma. Often served during Christmas, it’s accompanied by mashed rutabaga (kålrabistappe) and boiled potatoes. This hearty meal reflects Norway's culinary heritage, with its rich flavors and simple preparation rooted in preserving meat for the long winters. Pinnekjøtt is a festive favorite, cherished for its deep ties to Norwegian tradition and family gatherings.
